Escape to Boracay

Step into Boracay’s pristine beaches and vibrant waters. Whether you seek adventure or serenity, your dream vacation starts here.

Attractions

White Beach


White Beach is inarguably the most famous beach in Boracay and the most highlighted asset of Philippine tourism. The long stretch of fine white sand has captured the hearts of many travelers worldwide. It has repeatedly earned a place on numerous lists compiled by various international travel organizations and magazines.


White Beach dominates two-thirds of the western coast of Boracay. It is divided into three stations: Station 1 and 2 in Balabag and Station 3 in Manoc-Manoc. As the island’s most developed and popular area, business establishments such as accommodations, restaurants, bars, cafés, and souvenir shops line the coast. Station 1 mostly hosts luxury resorts, while Station 3 features budget lodgings. Most restaurants and shops are located in Station 2, making it the base for most tourists.


White Beach has quite the roster of activities for eager beachgoers — sunbathing, swimming, sunset watching, and water sports activities. It is also the main jump-off point for island-hopping tours and diving trips.

Puka Beach


No list of things to do on Boracay Island is complete without a visit to Puka Beach. Named for the puka shells that once lined its shores, Puka Beach offers a unique charm with its slightly coarser sand and almost untouched beauty. A lush forest perches atop the cliffs overlooking the beach, adding to its serene, natural ambiance, and with fewer establishments, it feels refreshingly peaceful.


Nestled on the northern tip of Boracay Island, Puka Beach is just a 20-minute tricycle ride from White Beach. Bring a mat and plan to stay awhile to fully enjoy this idyllic spot, which is included in most Boracay island hopping tours. Its distance from the main three stations means you’re likely to share this serene paradise with only a few others.


Puka Beach is also an ideal spot to catch a glimpse of Boracay Island’s majestic flying foxes. These fruit bats offer a spectacular sight during their migration, and you can help protect them by joining Friends of the Flying Foxes for a bat count.

Crystal Cove


Crystal Cove is one of Boracay’s most picturesque spots, located on the northern tip of the island. It is famous for its crystal-clear waters, dramatic limestone cliffs, and natural rock formations that create a unique and scenic landscape. The area is perfect for travelers who want to experience a quieter side of Boracay, away from the crowded beaches.


Visitors to Crystal Cove can explore its three main coves, each with its own charm. The caves and rock tunnels provide fun exploration opportunities, and the elevated viewpoints offer breathtaking panoramic views of the ocean. It’s also a popular spot for swimming, snorkeling, and capturing Instagram-worthy photos due to its natural beauty.


Besides its natural attractions, Crystal Cove promotes eco-tourism, ensuring that the area remains clean and preserved for future visitors. Local guides are available to provide insights about the island’s geology and marine life, enhancing the overall experience. Whether you’re seeking adventure, relaxation, or stunning landscapes, Crystal Cove is a must-visit destination in Boracay.

Cujo's Keyhole


Cujo’s Keyhole is a hidden gem on Boracay Island, known for its unique rock formations and secluded beach area. Its name comes from the natural “keyhole” arch formed by the cliffs, creating a picturesque and dramatic view that attracts photographers and nature lovers. The spot offers a peaceful escape from the busier tourist areas, making it ideal for relaxation and exploration.


Adventurous visitors can climb the surrounding rocks to reach the keyhole itself, which frames the sparkling turquoise waters of the sea perfectly. The area is also great for snorkeling and swimming in calm, clear waters. Its natural formation provides a sense of discovery, as each angle reveals a slightly different view of the ocean and the surrounding cliffs.


Cujo’s Keyhole is also part of Boracay’s efforts to promote eco-friendly tourism, so visitors are encouraged to keep the area clean and respect the environment. Local guides are often available to share interesting facts about the geological formations and marine life nearby.

Things To Do

Paraw Sailing

The paraw sailing adventure is a quintessential Boracay Island activity. A paraw is a local outrigger boat with two sails, the traditional way of navigating Boracay Island and its neighboring islands.

Banana Boat Ride

Joining a banana boat ride activity on Boracay Island is one of the most exciting experiences you can have on your vacation. Banana boats are giant yellow inflatable boats that can fit 10 to 20 people, perfect for groups of friends or family.

PARASAILING

Parasailing Boracay will give you one of the best views of the whole island, particularly of its coastline. It is definitely one of the most adrenaline-packed activities in Boracay!

Crystal Kayak

When it comes to travel photos of Boracay Island, there's nothing more Instagrammable than the trending crystal kayak ride. You will board a transparent kayak (commonly called a crystal kayak) where the turquoise-colored water of Boracay Island is seen in full glory.

Jet Ski

The exhilarating jet ski ride on Boracay Island is a must-try! It usually lasts for about 15 to 30 minutes. You'll be given the necessary instructions, such as the braking system, how to handle the throttle, and how to shut it off in case of an emergency.

Kiteboarding

If you're an adrenaline seeker, kiteboarding on Boracay Island is a thrilling way to experience the island's vibrant waters! This high-energy sport combines surfing with the power of a large kite, giving you the freedom to ride the waves with speed and skill.
