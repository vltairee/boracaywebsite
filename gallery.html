José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda

José Rizal was a Filipino doctor, writer, and nationalist born on June 19, 1861, in Calamba, Laguna. He became famous for his novels Noli Me Tángere and El Filibusterismo, which exposed injustices during Spanish rule in the Philippines. Through his writings and peaceful advocacy for reform, he inspired Filipinos to fight for freedom. He was executed on December 30, 1896, and is now considered the Philippines’ national hero.
